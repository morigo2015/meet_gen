# Legal Alignment Check

Checked on 2026-05-12 against current official sources available from Verkhovna Rada and the State Tax Service. This is a drafting checklist for sample documents, not legal advice.

Sources checked:

- Civil Code of Ukraine, current edition shown by Rada as of 2026-02-01: https://zakon.rada.gov.ua/laws/show/435-15
- Labor Code of Ukraine, current edition shown by Rada as of 2026-01-01: https://zakon.rada.gov.ua/laws/show/322-08
- Tax Code of Ukraine, current edition shown by Rada as of 2026-01-01: https://zakon.rada.gov.ua/laws/show/2755-17
- Law of Ukraine on unified social contribution, current edition shown by Rada as of 2026-01-26: https://zakon.rada.gov.ua/laws/show/2464-17
- State Tax Service guidance on civil-law contract remuneration and unified social contribution: https://kh.tax.gov.ua/media-ark/news-ark/792382.html

Template alignment notes:

1. Civil-law service model: the framework contract is structured as a paid service contract, with monthly requests defining the specific assignment and monthly acts confirming acceptance.
2. Monthly request workflow: each request specifies scope, expected result, acceptance criteria, deadline, and price; the act references both the framework contract and the request.
3. Labor-law risk control: the wording avoids job title as employment position, salary, fixed workplace, fixed working hours, vacation, sick leave, and internal labor rules. It states that the developer independently organizes service delivery.
4. IP rights: the framework contract and acts include assignment of economic IP rights to ordered software results after act signing and payment, while preserving personal non-property rights.
5. Tax/SSC handling: the sample assumes the contractor is an individual who is not an FOP; the customer acts as tax agent, withholds PIT and military levy from gross remuneration, and accrues SSC separately. Real cases must confirm contractor status and rates on the payment date.
6. Signature flow: every contract, request, and act has signature blocks for both parties.

Result of template-level check: no structural mismatch found against the above sources for a sample civil-law service workflow. Real production use still requires fact-specific legal and tax review.
